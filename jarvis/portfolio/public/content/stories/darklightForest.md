# The Darklight Forests

The Faewild and Shadowfell were never meant to touch. One is the mirror of all things vibrant, alive, an eternal celebration of nature's wonder. The other, a pit of shadows and dread, a place where life flees, never to return. But something had gone wrong. In an ancient ritual meant to restore balance, both planes merged simultaneously onto the Material Plane, neither dominant, both ever-present. The result was the Darklight Forest, a place where day and night shared the same breath, light and shadow intertwined like dancers in a macabre waltz.

The town that once thrived on the outskirts of this forest withered, the woods consuming it slowly from within. The few who remained lived in the eternal twilight, neither truly awake nor asleep, trapped in an eerie purgatory.

Drez stood by the door, methodically strapping on his tools, knives, crossbow, and other instruments for a hunt. His movements were precise, each action deliberate, but there was no rush. His weathered eyes passed over the muted gray walls of his small home. A home he had built with love once, but that now seemed like a hollow shell. He did not linger on the familiar sadness. Instead, he opened the door and stepped outside.

The town was a husk, drained of the life it once had. Houses leaned at odd angles, windows smashed inward as if something had crawled out of the depths of these homes to destroy them from within. The streets were littered with debris, evidence of attacks that had come from all directions. Bloodstains marred the cobblestone paths, some faded, some fresh.
As Drez walked, his footfalls were the only sound. Silence hung in the air like a curse. He passed the empty church, its doors wide open, as if waiting for lost souls to return. Inside, Father Maren still lit the candles every evening, clinging to some thread of hope. A young boy sat by the well, his legs dangling over the side, absently throwing rocks into the abyss. That boy was like a flicker of optimism in the void, but even that light was dimming.

In another corner of the town, Professor Ethra tinkered with her machines, trying to make sense of the chaos. Curiosity hadn't yet abandoned her, but her eyes spoke of countless sleepless nights, and the frantic 
desperation of needing answers in a world that no longer gave them.
The town was Drez, and Drez was the town. Desolate. Attacked from within and without.

He approached the forest, where light and shadow danced together in grotesque harmony. The canopy above seemed to sway as though whispering secrets to the wind, and the trees themselves looked... wrong. Some twisted unnaturally, others with bark that seemed to shimmer, neither alive nor dead. The ground beneath his feet pulsed, the very earth seeming to breathe in rhythm with the twisted world it held.
As Drez walked deeper, the calls of animals echoed through the trees, familiar yet somehow distorted. Their voices were disjointed, like echoes of creatures long dead. Every step Drez took was accompanied by the unsettling feeling that he was not alone. Shadows flickered across his path, unnatural movements that seemed to taunt him.

Suddenly, a weight pulled at him, something sharp but intangible. His own shadow rose up, twisting into something monstrous, attacking him. Drez stumbled back, slashing through it with his blade, but the feeling of being watched, hunted, deepened. And the darkness of the forest, which had once been a mere undercurrent, began to close in on him, suffocating.

Drez trudged forward, but the deeper he went, the slower he became. His steps felt heavy, as though the ground had turned to quicksand. The color around him drained away, the vibrancy of the forest flickering out like a dying candle. What remained was gray, muted, like the town he had just left.
Eyes blinked from the darkness, always just out of sight, watching him. He heard their whispers, soft, unintelligible, but they chilled his bones. And her name... Her name echoed through the trees, slipping between the murmurings like a thread of sorrow woven into the fabric of the forest. Drez lit a flare, the red light casting long shadows, but it did little. He caught a glimpse of something, distorted, wrong, hovering at the edge of his vision.

Suddenly, a burst of light cut through the shadows. One of the creatures of light, tiny and winged, collided with the flare. It exploded in a brilliant flash, illuminating the forest for a brief moment. Color flooded back, but the reprieve was short. The forest’s madness returned, brighter and more chaotic.

The light shifted erratically, casting the trees in twisted, vivid shapes. Pixies appeared, their faces stretched in wide, unsettling grins, flitting around Drez, worshiping him as if he were some god. They led him deeper, making him promises, asking only for the food he had left at home. In the mania of the moment, Drez agreed without hesitation.
He woke, hours later, to find himself surrounded by the bodies of pixies. Their tiny forms had been flayed open, arranged in a perfect circle around him, the forest sealing itself in as though mocking him. His heart pounded as he stared at the blood-red light reflecting off the trees, the path ahead a dark, narrow tunnel.

And there, at the end of it, he heard her voice. The one he had chased all this time.

The darkness swallowed him, thick and oppressive. It was as though he was wading through black water, each step dragging him deeper into despair. Voices whispered around him, offering him reasons to stop, to sit down, to give in.

But he pressed on, reaching the heart of the forest, where he found her, suspended in amber. She was trapped within the bark of a gnarled tree, her body encased in a crystal chrysalis. Her voice trembled, at once begging him to join her and pleading for him to run. She loved him. She despised him.

And then, the attacker came again, his own shadow, now a twisted mirror of himself, pulling him into a fight. Drez grappled with the dark version of his own soul, his blade flashing in the faint light. Just as he brought his weapon down for the final blow, he froze. The weapon was in his mouth, and the forest around him cackled with madness.

Drez stood before the amber prison, staring into her eyes. He could feel the weight of the gun in his hand, the decision hanging over him like the very air of the Darklight Forest.

The gun shifted.  
He fired. The amber cracked. She was free.  
Drez emerged from the forest with his shadow at his side, a silent companion on the path back to the hollow town.  
But nothing had truly changed.  
"What do I do now that my world is gone?"
